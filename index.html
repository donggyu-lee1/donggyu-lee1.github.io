About

Research rooted in real-world measurement

I am currently pursuing an M.S. in Data Science at KAIST. My research focuses on how machine learning can help measure, interpret, and reason about socio-economic phenomena, especially when reliable data are limited or unevenly distributed.

Across my recent work, I have studied satellite-imagery-based economic estimation, AI-assisted monitoring of development disparities, and the strengths and limits of large language models in economic causal reasoning. I am especially interested in research that connects rigorous empirical questions with practical tools for policy and social impact.

Previously, I worked as a research intern at the Max Planck Institute for Security and Privacy and as a machine learning intern at Samsung Electronics. I also studied computer science at KAIST, with a minor in economics, and spent a semester abroad at EPFL.

Research

Current interests

I am interested in methodological work that stays grounded in socially meaningful questions and usable measurements.

ML for Economics & Finance

Using machine learning to model socio-economic indicators, economic disparities, and causal structure in high-stakes domains.

Computational Social Science

Studying social and institutional systems with data-driven methods that support interpretable empirical reasoning.

Satellite Imagery & Remote Sensing

Combining geospatial analysis, satellite imagery, and AI to estimate development and economic conditions where surveys are scarce.

Project Snapshot

Regional Economic Indicator Estimation with LLMs

In collaboration with Samsung Electronics, I worked on an end-to-end pipeline for estimating subnational economic indicators in European countries using satellite imagery, geospatial preprocessing, and large language models.
